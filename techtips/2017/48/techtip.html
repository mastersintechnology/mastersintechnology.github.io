Automatic Bibliography in Microsoft Word

Plagiarism is a type of academic dishonesty. To avoid it, always cite your sources and include a bibliography at the end of your work. Word has a built-in citation and bibliography feature.

In the 'References' tab, you can click 'Insert citation' to cite a source to support a statement you make.



You get a window asking for information about the source. Select the most appropriate source type from the dropdown and fill any fields you can.


To view all your sources, click the 'Citations' button in the 'References' tab.


The Master Source list contains all your citations across all documents so you can share citations between works. You can edit this list (if, for example, you don't want to have this universal list) by clicking the gear button and clicking 'Citation Source Manager...'


Word supports several citation formats. Click the dropdown in the citation list and pick a format to automatically update all your in-text citations to use that format.


In the 'References' tab, click the 'Bibliography' button to insert a bibliography that includes all the sources in the current document.
